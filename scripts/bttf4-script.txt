## CHARACTER PREAMBLES

**MARTY McFLY** - Now 47 years old, Marty has become a successful musician and inventor in his own right. He's married to Jennifer (formerly Jennifer Parker), and they have two teenage children. Marty carries the wisdom of his time-traveling experiences but struggles with the temptation to "fix" problems using knowledge of the future. He's protective of his family and haunted by the responsibility of keeping the time travel secret.

**JENNIFER McFLY** - Formerly Jennifer Parker, now 47, she's a successful architect who designed the McFly family's modern home. She knows about Marty's time-traveling past but has never experienced it herself. She's practical, grounded, and serves as Marty's anchor to the present. Jennifer is concerned about their children's futures and the pressure they feel living up to the McFly legacy.

**MARLENE McFLY** - 17 years old, Marty and Jennifer's daughter. Brilliant like her grandfather George but rebellious like her father was at that age. She's a gifted student with a passion for theoretical physics, having grown up hearing stories about "Uncle Doc Brown." She suspects there's more to her family's history than they're telling her.

**MARTY Jr.** - 15 years old, Marty's son who struggles with his father's shadow. He's artistic but lacks confidence, much like George McFly was in the original timeline. He feels pressure to live up to the McFly name and is desperate to prove himself worthy of his family's mysterious legacy.

**DR. EMMETT L. "DOC" BROWN** - Now 95 years old, having aged naturally since returning from his future travels. He's brilliant as ever but more frail, using a high-tech walker he's designed himself. He's married to Clara Clayton-Brown and has been living quietly, but the call of scientific discovery still burns within him. He serves as the wise mentor figure, keeper of time travel secrets.

**CLARA CLAYTON-BROWN** - Now in her 80s, Doc's wife from the Old West. She's adapted remarkably well to modern times and often serves as the voice of reason when Doc gets too excited about scientific possibilities. She's a loving grandmother figure to the McFly children.

**BIFF TANNEN III** - 17 years old, great-grandson of the original Biff. Unlike his ancestor, he's intelligent and well-mannered, but he carries the family's ambitious streak. He's Marlene's classmate and represents the complexity of generational change - not inherently evil, but potentially dangerous if given too much power.

**GRIFF TANNEN** - 45 years old, Biff III's father and grandson of Griff from 2015. He's a tech entrepreneur who's built a fortune in quantum computing. He represents the modern evolution of the Tannen family - successful but still harboring resentment about his family's historical "failures."

## FADE IN:

### EXT. HILL VALLEY - TOWN SQUARE - DAY (OCTOBER 21, 2015)

*The clock tower still shows 10:04 - it's been repaired since the lightning strike but maintains its historical significance. The town square bustles with activity, but it's different from both 1985 and the 2015 we saw in Part II. This is the real 2015 - smartphones, electric cars, but no flying vehicles or hoverboards.*

*A bronze statue stands in the center of the square: "IN MEMORY OF DR. EMMETT L. BROWN - SCIENTIST, INVENTOR, FRIEND." But we can see DOC BROWN himself, aged 95, sitting on a bench nearby, chuckling at his own memorial.*

### INT. HILL VALLEY HIGH SCHOOL - PHYSICS CLASS - DAY

*The same school, but updated with modern technology. Interactive whiteboards, tablets, but the basic structure remains. MR. STRICKLAND III teaches physics - he looks remarkably like his grandfather but with a kinder demeanor.*

**STRICKLAND III**  
Now, class, who can tell me about temporal paradoxes in quantum mechanics?

*MARLENE McFLY's hand shoots up. She's 17, with Jennifer's features but Marty's determined spirit and George's intellectual curiosity.*

**MARLENE**  
A temporal paradox occurs when information or objects exist in a causal loop with no discernible origin - like the bootstrap paradox, where effect precedes cause.

**STRICKLAND III**  
Excellent, Miss McFly. Though of course, time travel remains purely theoretical...

*Marlene exchanges a knowing glance with BIFF TANNEN III, who sits nearby. He's handsome, intelligent, and nothing like his great-grandfather - which somehow makes him more dangerous.*

**BIFF III**  
(raising his hand)  
But Mr. Strickland, what if time travel were possible? Wouldn't the ability to change the past give someone incredible power?

*The question hangs in the air. Marlene shifts uncomfortably.*

**STRICKLAND III**  
An interesting thought experiment, Mr. Tannen. But such power would be incredibly dangerous in the wrong hands.

### EXT. HILL VALLEY HIGH SCHOOL - LATER

*Marlene walks out with her backpack, lost in thought. MARTY Jr. catches up - he's 15, artistic, carrying a sketchbook. He has his father's features but lacks confidence.*

**MARTY Jr.**  
Hey, sis. Dad's picking us up today. Something about a "family meeting."

**MARLENE**  
(suspicious)  
Family meeting? We never have family meetings unless...

*A pristine black TESLA MODEL S pulls up. MARTY McFLY, now 47, gets out. He's successful, confident, but there's worry in his eyes. He wears a casual blazer over a vintage band t-shirt - still cool, but grown up.*

**MARTY**  
Hey, kids. Hop in.

**MARLENE**  
Dad, what's this about? You look like you've seen a ghost.

**MARTY**  
(forcing a smile)  
Nothing to worry about. Just... family stuff.

*As they drive away, we see GRIFF TANNEN watching from a modern office building across the street. He's 45, successful, wearing an expensive suit. He speaks into a Bluetooth earpiece.*

**GRIFF**  
(into phone)  
They're moving. Activate phase two.

### INT. TESLA MODEL S - CONTINUOUS

*The car is quiet except for the hum of the electric motor. Marlene studies her father in the rearview mirror.*

**MARLENE**  
Dad, I've been thinking about Grandpa George's old stories. The ones about the "spaceman" who visited him in 1955?

*Marty's knuckles tighten on the steering wheel.*

**MARTY**  
Your grandfather had a very active imagination.

**MARTY Jr.**  
But the stories always matched up perfectly. Same details every time. And what about Doc Brown? Why does everyone say he died in 1985, but I swear I've seen an old man who looks just like him around town?

**MARTY**  
(too quickly)  
People see what they want to see.

*Marlene and Marty Jr. exchange glances. They're not buying it.*

### EXT. McFLY HOUSE - DAY

*The McFly house is now a beautiful modern home - clearly designed by an architect (Jennifer). It maintains some classic elements but with clean lines and solar panels. The DeLorean is nowhere to be seen, but there's a large garage with tinted windows.*

*JENNIFER McFLY emerges as they pull up. She's 47, successful, beautiful, but concerned. She's wearing architect's clothing - practical but stylish.*

**JENNIFER**  
(to Marty, quietly)  
Doc called. Three times. He says it's urgent.

**MARTY**  
(to the kids)  
Go inside. We'll be right there.

*The kids reluctantly head inside.*

**JENNIFER**  
Marty, what's happening?

**MARTY**  
I think someone's figured it out. About the time travel.

**JENNIFER**  
Who?

**MARTY**  
I'm not sure yet. But Doc found some... irregularities in the timeline. Small changes that weren't there before.

**JENNIFER**  
What kind of changes?

**MARTY**  
The kind that suggest someone else has been messing with time.

### INT. McFLY HOUSE - LIVING ROOM - DAY

*The interior is modern but comfortable. Family photos line the walls, including some suspicious ones - like Marty with what appears to be 1950s clothing, or a group photo that includes someone who looks remarkably like a younger Doc Brown in old-fashioned dress.*

*Marlene is examining these photos when her parents enter with DOC BROWN and CLARA CLAYTON-BROWN. Doc is 95, using a high-tech walker, but his eyes are still sharp. Clara is in her 80s, dignified and warm.*

**DOC**  
Marty, Jennifer, we need to tell them.

**MARTY**  
Doc, we agreed-

**DOC**  
That was before someone else started playing with time! The space-time continuum is being altered, and if we don't act soon-

**MARLENE**  
(standing up)  
Tell us what?

*The adults exchange glances.*

**CLARA**  
(gently)  
Children, what we're about to tell you will sound impossible.

**DOC**  
Your father is a time traveler.

*Beat. The kids stare.*

**MARTY Jr.**  
(laughing)  
Okay, Dad, very funny. Is this about my grades?

**MARLENE**  
(more serious)  
Wait. You're serious.

**MARTY**  
(sighing)  
Kids, sit down.

*They do, reluctantly.*

**MARTY**  
In 1985, when I was your age, Marlene, I accidentally traveled back in time to 1955. I met your grandfather when he was seventeen, and I... well, I accidentally prevented your grandparents from falling in love.

**MARLENE**  
That's impossible.

**DOC**  
(pulling out a familiar photograph)  
Is it?

*He shows them the photograph from the original film - the one of Marty, Dave, and Linda at the "Enchantment Under the Sea" dance.*

**DOC**  
This is your father in 1955, at your grandparents' high school dance.

*Marlene takes the photo, studies it intently.*

**MARLENE**  
(whispered)  
Oh my God. Dad, you look exactly like this in your old yearbook photos, but this is clearly from the 1950s. The cars, the clothes, the image quality...

**MARTY Jr.**  
But time travel is impossible! Isn't it?

**DOC**  
I invented a time machine using a DeLorean DMC-12. We've made several trips through time, preventing disasters and fixing temporal anomalies.

**JENNIFER**  
And now we think someone else has figured out how to time travel.

**CLARA**  
Someone who might not have the same... ethical constraints.

*Suddenly, the lights flicker. Electronic devices start glitching.*

**DOC**  
(checking a device from his walker)  
Electromagnetic pulse! Everyone down!

*They duck as a BRIGHT FLASH illuminates the windows.*

### EXT. McFLY HOUSE - CONTINUOUS

*A sleek vehicle that's clearly not from 2015 - it has the design aesthetic of something from 2035 or beyond - hovers above the street. It's making the same electrical humming sound as the DeLorean's time circuits.*

*GRIFF TANNEN steps out, but he's different now - his clothes have changed to something more futuristic, and he carries a device that looks like an advanced version of Doc's equipment.*

**GRIFF**  
(calling out)  
McFly! I know you're in there! And I know what you've been hiding!

### INT. McFLY HOUSE - LIVING ROOM - CONTINUOUS

*Everyone is on the floor. Doc checks his readings.*

**DOC**  
(urgent whisper)  
Time traveler. Definitely not from this era.

**MARTY**  
(to his kids)  
Stay down. Don't move.

**MARLENE**  
(whispering)  
Dad, I'm scared.

**JENNIFER**  
(to Marty)  
The DeLorean?

**MARTY**  
In the garage. But if he's got time travel technology...

**DOC**  
Wait. These readings... this isn't just time travel. This is something new. Something that shouldn't exist yet.

**GRIFF**  
(from outside, amplified)  
Forty-seven years, McFly! Forty-seven years I've been stuck cleaning up your family's messes! But not anymore!

*The front door EXPLODES inward. Griff enters, but his appearance keeps shifting slightly - as if he's not entirely stable in this timeline.*

**GRIFF**  
(to the family)  
Did you really think you could change the past without consequences? Did you think the Tannen family would just accept being your employees forever?

**MARTY**  
(standing slowly, hands visible)  
Griff, whatever you think you know-

**GRIFF**  
I know everything! I know about 1885, when you humiliated my great-great-grandfather. I know about 1955, when you turned him into a loser. I know about 2015, when you prevented my grandfather from achieving his destiny!

**DOC**  
(standing with his walker)  
Young man, temporal manipulation for personal gain is-

**GRIFF**  
Shut up, old man! You created this technology and kept it for yourselves! Well, not anymore.

*He pulls out a device that looks like a futuristic version of the DeLorean's time circuits, but handheld.*

**GRIFF**  
I've spent thirty years developing my own time travel technology. And unlike you, I'm not afraid to use it properly.

**MARLENE**  
(standing up bravely)  
What do you want?

**GRIFF**  
(smiling coldly)  
I want to fix what your father broke. I want to restore the natural order - with the Tannens in charge and the McFlys in their proper place.

**MARTY Jr.**  
Dad, is he serious? Can he really-?

**GRIFF**  
Oh, I'm very serious. And I'm going to start by ensuring you McFly children never exist at all.

*He activates his device. The room fills with the familiar white light of time travel.*

**DOC**  
(shouting over the noise)  
Everyone grab hands! If he's creating a temporal vortex-

*But it's too late. The white light engulfs everything.*

### INT. TEMPORAL VOID - CONTINUOUS

*Everyone is suspended in the white, swirling energy of time travel. But this is different from the DeLorean's smooth transitions - this is chaotic, unstable.*

**GRIFF**  
(his voice echoing strangely)  
Welcome to the space between seconds, McFly! This is where I decide your fate!

**MARLENE**  
(calling out to her father)  
Dad! What's happening?

**MARTY**  
(reaching for his children)  
Kids! Hold on!

*But the temporal energy is pulling them in different directions.*

**DOC**  
(analyzing readings on a device)  
This isn't normal time travel! He's fracturing the timeline!

*Suddenly, multiple versions of familiar locations flash around them - the Hill Valley town square in different eras, the McFly house at different times, the high school across decades.*

**GRIFF**  
I'm going to scatter your family across time, McFly. Let's see you try to fix this paradox!

*The energy reaches a crescendo. Everyone is pulled in different directions.*

**MARTY**  
(desperately)  
Marlene! Marty Jr.!

**MARLENE**  
Dad!

**MARTY Jr.**  
Help us!

*The white light explodes outward.*

### FADE TO BLACK

### INT. UNKNOWN LOCATION - DAY

*MARLENE opens her eyes. She's lying on grass. She sits up slowly, looking around. She's in what appears to be Hill Valley's town square, but something's wrong.*

*The architecture is different - more advanced, but in a 1950s retrofuturistic way. Flying cars move overhead, but they're sleek chrome vehicles that look like they were designed in 1955. People walk by in a blend of 1950s and futuristic fashion.*

*A newspaper blows past. The headline reads: "TANNEN INDUSTRIES CELEBRATES 60 YEARS OF INNOVATION - 2015"*

*But the date on the paper says "OCTOBER 21, 2015" - today's date.*

**MARLENE**  
(to herself)  
This isn't right. This isn't our 2015.

*She looks at the clock tower. Instead of reading 10:04, it shows the correct time. And instead of a memorial to Doc Brown, there's a statue of BIFF TANNEN with the inscription: "BIFF TANNEN - VISIONARY LEADER OF HILL VALLEY."*

**MARLENE**  
(realizing)  
He did it. He changed the timeline.

*She stands up, brushing herself off. She needs to find her family, but more importantly, she needs to figure out how to fix this.*

*As she walks down the street, she sees a familiar storefront: "BROWN'S SCIENTIFIC INSTRUMENTS - CLOSED BY ORDER OF TANNEN INDUSTRIES."*

*A faded sign in the window shows a much younger Doc Brown with the words: "DANGEROUS INVENTOR - DO NOT TRUST."*

**MARLENE**  
(determined)  
Okay, Griff. If you want to play with time, let's play.

*She pulls out her smartphone, but instead of her normal interface, the screen shows a strange temporal readout - somehow, her phone is picking up temporal energy.*

**MARLENE**  
(realizing)  
I'm not just in the wrong timeline. I'm still connected to the temporal fracture.

*She looks at the readings. Multiple blips show on different time periods.*

**MARLENE**  
(to herself)  
Dad, Marty Jr., everyone... we're all scattered across time. But if I can figure out Dad's old tricks...

*She starts walking toward where the McFly house should be, but stops when she sees the street sign: "TANNEN AVENUE."*

**MARLENE**  
(grimly)  
This is going to be harder than I thought.

### FADE TO:

### TITLE CARD: "BACK TO THE FUTURE IV: DESTINY"

### FADE TO BLACK.

## END OF SCENE 1

**TO BE CONTINUED...**

*This opening establishes the new status quo while honoring the original films' themes. Marlene becomes our new protagonist - she has the intelligence to understand the science, the courage to take action, and the family legacy to motivate her. The scattered family across different time periods sets up multiple storylines while maintaining the personal stakes that made the original films work.*

*Key elements preserved:*
- *Fish out of water dynamics (Marlene in wrong timeline)*
- *Personal family stakes (family scattered across time)*
- *Unintended consequences (Griff's revenge creates new problems)*
- *Visual time period contrasts (corrupted 2015)*
- *The theme of destiny vs. choice*

*New elements introduced:*
- *Next generation taking the lead*
- *Technology evolved beyond the DeLorean*
- *Consequences of the original trilogy's changes*
- *The complexity of generational change*

*The stage is now set for Marlene to discover her own time-travel abilities, find her scattered family members across different eras, and ultimately confront the deeper question: Is it possible to truly "fix" the past, or do all attempts to change history simply create new problems for the next generation to solve?*
